Biography TimeLine

Derrick Henry is an American football running back who plays for the Tennessee Titans of the National Football League (NFL). He was born on January 4, 1994, in Yulee, Florida. Here is a detailed biography timeline of his life and career.

1994: Derrick Henry was born in Yulee, Florida.

2013: Henry graduated from Yulee High School, setting the national high school rushing record with 12,124 career yards.

2013-2014: Henry attended the University of Alabama and played for the Alabama Crimson Tide football team.

2015: The Tennessee Titans drafted Henry in the second round of the NFL Draft.

2016: Henry made his NFL debut on September 11, 2016, against the Minnesota Vikings. He finished the season with 490 rushing yards and five touchdowns.

2017: Henry played in all 16 games for the Titans, rushing for 744 yards and five touchdowns.

2018: Henry rushed for a career-high 1,059 yards and 12 touchdowns, including a record-breaking performance against the Jacksonville Jaguars on December 6, when he rushed for 238 yards and four touchdowns.

2019: Henry had his best season, rushing for 1,540 yards and 16 touchdowns. He led the league in rushing and was named to the Pro Bowl.

2020: Henry continued his dominant play, leading the league in rushing for the second consecutive year with 2,027 yards and 17 touchdowns. He was named First-Team All-Pro and won the NFL Offensive Player of the Year award.

2021: Henry suffered a season-ending foot injury on October 31, 2021, against the Indianapolis Colts.
