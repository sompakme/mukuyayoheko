Ethan Torchio Biography – Ethan Torchio Wiki

Ethan Torchio is an Italian Drummer and a member of Italy’s Eurovision-Winning Band, The Måneskin. The Måneskin is an Italian rock band consisting of members Damiano David (lead vocals), Victoria De Angeli (bass), Thomas Raggi (guitar), and Ethan (drums). Måneskin, Danish for “moonlight” as a tribute to bassist Victoria De Angelis’s Danish heritage. The band music band was formed in 2016 in Rome Italy. Måneskin rose to fame after their debut single “Chosen” charted at number 2 on the Italian FIMI Singles Chart, and was later performed as covers during the X Factor live shows in 2017. The band continued to release several singles, many of which had peak chart positions of 1st or 2nd place, and also released an album that reached number 1 in Italy and 26 in Switzerland.

Ethan appeared in the feature documentary This is Måneskin, in 2018. In March 2021, the band competed in the 71st edition of the annual Sanremo Music Festival held in the Teatro Ariston of Sanremo. With their song “Zitti e buoni”, the band emerged winner of the “Big Artists” section of the contest, which saw the participation of 26 artists. In May 2021, the group won the Eurovision competition in Rotterdam, the Netherlands, with their tune “Zitti E Buoni,” which translates into English as “shut up and be quiet.” The band’s win marked Italy’s third victory since the competition began in 1956. The first win was in 1964, by Gigliola Cinquett with the song “Non Ho L’Età,” and the second was in 1990, when Toto Cutugno won for “Insieme 1992.”
