It has been a year since the sudden and unexpected death of actor Ray Liotta, best known for his roles in films such as Goodfellas and Field of Dreams.

On May 26th, 2022, Liotta died in his sleep while working on a movie in the Dominican Republic.

After months of speculation, an autopsy report has finally revealed the cause of death: cardiovascular disease.

The 67-year-old actor was found to have suffered from pulmonary edema and acute heart failure due to atherosclerotic disease.

According to documents obtained by TMZ, the manner of death was categorized as natural and nonviolent, citing respiratory insufficiency, cardiac arrest and heart failure as contributing factors.

Liotta had been filming a movie called Dangerous Waters when he passed away.

His publicist released a statement saying that he had “enjoyed a successful career spanning four decades” and “was looking forward to continuing his work in film” before his untimely death.

The news of Liotta’s passing sent shockwaves through Hollywood and beyond.

He was beloved by fans for his iconic performances in films such as Goodfellas and Field of Dreams, both of which earned him critical acclaim.

In addition to being an accomplished actor, Liotta was also an avid philanthropist who supported numerous charities throughout his life.

Ray Liotta’s death is a huge loss for the entertainment industry; he will be remembered for his memorable performances on screen as well as his generous spirit offscreen.

His legacy will live on through all those who were touched by him during his lifetime.
