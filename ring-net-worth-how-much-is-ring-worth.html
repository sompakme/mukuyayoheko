The History of Ring

Ring LLC, formerly known as Doorbot, was founded in 2013 by Jamie Siminoff as a crowdfunded startup. The company initially raised $364,000 USD to develop its groundbreaking smart doorbell technology. In 2014, the company rebranded as Ring and launched its second-generation hardware, which gained widespread attention and popularity.

Ring’s innovative concept of a video doorbell revolutionized home security by allowing users to monitor and communicate with visitors at their front door remotely through a smartphone app. The company quickly gained recognition for its sleek design, ease of use, and advanced features.

In 2016, Ring secured a significant equity stake from retired NBA superstar Shaquille O’Neal and raised over $200 million USD in investments, further fueling its growth and expansion plans. Two years later, in 2018, Ring caught the attention of e-commerce giant Amazon, which acquired the company for an estimated value between $1.2 and $1.8 billion USD. The acquisition enabled Ring to leverage Amazon’s resources and global reach to further develop and market its products.

The history of Ring showcases the company’s evolution from a humble startup to a leading player in the smart home security market. With its continuous innovation and strategic partnerships, Ring has established itself as a trusted brand that delivers innovative and reliable solutions for homeowners worldwide.
