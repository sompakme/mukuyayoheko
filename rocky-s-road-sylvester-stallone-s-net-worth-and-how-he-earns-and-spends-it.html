He crafted his own destiny by writing and starring in the 1976 boxing underdog classic, Rocky, catapulting himself from obscurity to superstardom.

Little did he know this wouldn't be a one-hit wonder. Rocky spawned six sequels, each a box office success, and cemented Stallone's action-hero image.

But he wasn't a one-man show. Stallone's Vietnam War veteran, John Rambo, became another historical action figure, generating his own five-film franchise and further solidifying his place as a box office magnet.

How Sylvester Stallone Earns His Money

Stallone's Hollywood Blockbusters

Between the 1970s and 2012, Stallone's base film salaries alone totaled $300 million, equivalent to approximately $500 million today after adjusting for inflation.

Sylvester Stallone's first stride in Hollywood was a modest $200 paycheck for his first starring role in The Party At Kitty And Stud's, driven by desperation during a period of homelessness.

Stallone rose to prominence with Rocky, a film that changed Stallone's life. He wrote the screenplay three days after watching the Muhammad Ali vs. Chuck Wepner fight in 1975:

Stallone initially had to fight to take the lead role. His persistence paid off after he came to an agreement with the United Artists studio executives to accept a significant pay cut and a production budget of $1 million.

According to Variety, Stallone earned $35,000 for the 25-day shoot, combining his writing and acting pay. Factoring in backend percentages and ten net points, MGM's total payment to Stallone for Rocky amounts to around $2.5 million.

While he missed out on Rocky's lucrative merchandising rights, this underdog tale became his launchpad. With each Rocky sequel, Stallone's star power and paychecks grew.

Rocky II brought Stallone a $75,000 payday, and the third installment, Rocky III, doubled it to $120,000. He was paid $15 million by Rocky V and later returned to the franchise for Creed I and Creed II with $10 million base salaries each.

However, Rocky wasn't his only cash cow. His Rambo franchise also proved lucrative, with Stallone earning $3.5 million for First Blood and $16 million for Rambo III.

Beyond his signature franchises, Stallone consistently negotiated impressive paychecks. In 1983, the Rambo actor earned $10 million (equivalent to $25 million adjusted for inflation) for his debut as a producer with Staying Alive.

In 1984, he earned $4 million and a producer credit for Rhinestone. Maintaining a strong paycheck, Stallone received $12 million for Over the Top.

Films like Lock Up, Tango & Cash, and Demolition Man all netted him between $15 and $16 million each, while Daybreak garnered him $17.5 million, and Driven and Eye See You brought in $20 million apiece.

Stallone cashed in a total of $51 million from The Expendables franchise. For the first three films, where he served as co-writer, producer, and star, Stallone received around $16 million for each, totaling $48 million, according to Parade.

In the fourth installment, with a reduced role, he took a salary of $3 million. In 2021, Stallone lent his voice to King Shark in James Gunn's soft reboot, The Suicide Squad, earning a reported $3 million for his vocal contribution.

Stallone's foray into television was as critically acclaimed as his big screen efforts. In 2022, Stallone took his first-ever leading role in a scripted television series with Tulsa King.

His television debut saw him earn $1 million per episode, as the crime saga proved a hit with viewers.

Stallone's Production Studio

Sylvester Stallone ventured into film production with Balboa Productions, co-founded with Braden Aftergood in March 2018.

The studio signed a multi-year collaboration deal with Starlight Culture Entertainment to develop projects for film and television.

This collaboration hints at the studio's willingness to explore beyond Stallone's signature action genre, potentially venturing into new cinematic territories.

While still young, the studio has produced some heavy hitters. Rambo: Last Blood, the fifth installment in the Rambo franchise, grossed a respectable $91.5 million globally, exceeding its $50 million budget:

The production company also produced Creed III, the third installment in the Creed film series and the ninth overall in the Rocky film series, which was an even bigger success.

According to Box Office Mojo, Creed III grossed $156.3 million through the domestic box office and $119 million in other territories, for a global total of $275.3 million.
